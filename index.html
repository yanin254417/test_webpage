Abstract

   Course Scheduling helps schools meet their academic objectives according to pre-established plans. Many schools still rely on human labor for course scheduling. Scheduling courses involves solving a complex problem that requires addressing numerous constraints such as student and teacher availability, classroom capacity, student enrollment, teacher preferences for teaching times, and more. Hence, this study develops a mathematical model to generate course timetables for teachers, students, and classrooms in the Department of Statistics at Khon Kaen University. The program can handle course scheduling for both Bachelor of Science and Master of Science degrees. To efficiently and easily arrange course schedules Course scheduling employs Integer Linear Programming (ILP) to meet both hard and soft constraints. These constraints are essential for developing an optimal and comprehensive course timetable with the objective of maximizing the number of courses allocated in the timetable.
